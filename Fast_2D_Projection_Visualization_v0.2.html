Projection method — The electron scattering factor of each atom is parameterised as a sum of 5 Gaussians using Peng 1999 tabulated coefficients: f(s) = Σᵢ aᵢ exp(−bᵢ s²), where s = sinθ/λ (Å⁻¹) is the Peng scattering parameter (= 1/(2d) for Bragg spacing d), aᵢ (Å) and bᵢ (Å²) are element-specific coefficients fit to electron diffraction data. The 2D projected density per atom is obtained analytically from the 2D inverse Fourier transform of each Gaussian slice (projection-slice theorem): φᵢ(ρ) = (π aᵢ / beff,i) exp(−π²ρ² / beff,i), where ρ is the 2D radial distance from the atom centre. The Blur σ² slider adds a constant σ² (Å²) to every Peng width: beff,i = bi + σ², widening each Gaussian and suppressing high-frequency detail. σ² = 0 gives the exact Peng potential; larger σ² progressively blurs fine features. A 3D voxel volume is cached after loading; during rotation a fast ray-cast is used, and a full per-atom sum is recomputed on release. Grid spacing ≥ 1 Å/px. Box Grid sets the canvas pixel resolution (128–512 px); larger grids give sharper images at the cost of compute time. Box Size controls the field of view as a multiple of the longest molecular dimension.
